Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Animalia --- Multicellular, eukaryotic organisms

  • Phylum: Chordatan --- Animals that have a notochord, a hollow dorsal nerve cord, an endostyle or thyroid, pharyngeal slits, and a post-anal tail at some point during their larval or adult stages

  • Class: Mammalia --- Vertebrate animals that characterised by the presence of milk-producing mammary glands for feeding their young, a broad neocortex region of the brain, fur or hair, and three middle ear bones

  • Order: Carnivora --- Placental mammals specialized primarily in eating flesh

  • Suborder: Caniformia --- "Dog-like" carnivorans of which most members have a long snout and nonretractile claws

  • Family: Canidae --- The family includes three subfamilies (Caninae, Borophaginae & Hesperocyoninae) and include domestic dogs, wolves, coyotes, raccoon dogs, foxes, jackals and other species

  • Subfamily: Caninae --- The only living subfamily within Canidae

  • Genus: Canis --- Species of this genus (eg. wolves, dogs, coyotes, and golden jackal) are characterized by moderate to large size, their massive, well-developed skulls and dentition, long legs, and comparatively short ears and tails

  • Species: C. lupus --- Gray wolf

    • General Discription

    Gray wolf is the largest wild extant member of the Canidae(dog) family. Compared to other Canis species, it has less pointed ears and snout, a shorter torso, larger legs, and larger feet. The fur color of gray wolves is usually mottled gray, but it can also be pure white or black.
